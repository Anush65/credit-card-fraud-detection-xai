Transaction Scenarios

Real-world examples of how our AI distinguishes between fraud and normal activity.

The "Testing" Charge

Scenario: A thief verifies a stolen card with a tiny $1.00 transaction before draining the account.

AI Signals: Extremely small amount coupled with a mismatch in "Digital Fingerprint" (V4, V12) indicating a new, unverified device.

The "Impossible Traveler"

Scenario: Card used in New York at 9:00 AM and then in London at 9:15 AM.

AI Signals: The time difference vs. location change is physically impossible. This causes a massive spike in V17 and V10 deviation.

The "Burst Attack"

Scenario: Multiple high-value electronics purchases made within 5 minutes online.

AI Signals: Rapid succession of high amounts (Amount, Time) combined with unusual V14 patterns triggers an immediate alert.

The "Morning Coffee"

Scenario: You buy coffee for $5.50 at the same shop every weekday morning.

AI Signals: Consistent location, time, and amount. The "Digital Fingerprint" (V-features) matches your established history perfectly.

Subscription Renewal

Scenario: A monthly Netflix charge of $15.99 on the 10th of every month.

AI Signals: Highly predictable pattern. Even if the amount is slightly higher, the recurring nature flags this as safe.

Holiday Travel

Scenario: You buy dinner in Paris while on vacation, far from home.

AI Signals: Although location is new, the spending behavior (V-features) matches your typical restaurant visits, avoiding a false alarm.

What are V4, V10, V17...?

They are "Digital Fingerprints"

To protect your privacy, banks do not share your Name, Address, or exact Location in the dataset. Instead, they run a mathematical process called PCA (Principal Component Analysis).

This compresses all your private data (User ID, IP Address, GPS, Device Type) into abstract numbers like V1, V2... V28.

Important for Users:

You cannot measure or calculate these numbers yourself! They are encrypted mathematical representations.

When using the Detector, please use the "Load Scenario" buttons to simulate how these values look for different real-world events.
